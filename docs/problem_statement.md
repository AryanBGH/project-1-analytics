# Problem Statement

## Context
This dataset represents customer-level information for a subscription-based service.
Each record corresponds to an individual customer and includes demographic attributes,
service usage behavior, support interactions, payment delays, subscription type,
contract length, and total spend.

Customer retention is a critical business concern because acquiring new customers
is typically more expensive than retaining existing ones. Understanding patterns
in customer behavior can help the business identify risk indicators and prioritize
retention actions more effectively.

## Stakeholder & Decision
The primary stakeholders are business, product, and operations leaders responsible
for customer retention and revenue stability.

The key decision this analysis supports is:
**Which customer segments exhibit higher risk indicators related to churn, and what
actions should be prioritized to improve customer retention?**

## Key Questions
This analysis aims to answer the following questions:
1. Are there identifiable customer segments that show higher churn risk indicators?
2. How do usage frequency, support calls, and payment delays relate to potential churn risk?
3. Do subscription type and contract length influence customer stability?
4. Which behavioral or contractual factors appear most strongly associated with at-risk customers?

## Success Criteria
The analysis will be considered successful if it:
- Identifies clear customer segments with elevated churn risk indicators
- Produces interpretable, decision-oriented insights rather than technical metrics
- Supports prioritization of retention strategies for business stakeholders

## Constraints
- Python execution is not available in the local environment
- Analysis is limited to exploratory and descriptive techniques
- No predictive modeling or machine learning is performed in this project
- All results are documented transparently without false execution claims
